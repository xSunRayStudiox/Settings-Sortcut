Accessing Manage Application

  1. Open Settings: Start by opening the Settings app on your Android device.
  2. Navigate to Apps & Notifications: Scroll down and select "Apps & notifications" or simply "Apps" depending on your device.
  3. View All Apps: Tap on "See all apps" or "App info" to see the list of all installed applications.

Managing Individual App Settings

  1. Select an App: From the list of apps, select the app you want to manage.
  2. App Info: You will see the App Info screen with various options to manage the app settings.
  3. Force Stop: Stops the app from running. Use this if an app is unresponsive or behaving abnormally.
  4. Uninstall: Removes the app from your device. Note that some pre-installed apps cannot be uninstalled but can be disabled.
  5. Storage & Cache: Manage the app's storage by clearing its cache or data.
  6. Permissions: Adjust the permissions granted to the app, such as access to the camera, location, or contacts.
  7. Notifications: Customize how the app sends notifications, including turning them off entirely.
  8. Data Usage: View and manage the app's data usage, including background data restrictions.

Additional Settings

  1. Special App Access: In the "Apps & notifications" menu, select "Advanced" and then "Special app access" to manage specific permissions like battery optimization, device admin apps, and more.
  2. Default Apps: You can set default apps for actions like browsing, messaging, and calling by selecting "Default apps" in the "Apps & notifications" menu.
Note: Changing app settings can affect how apps behave on your device. Make sure to understand the impact of each setting before making changes.
