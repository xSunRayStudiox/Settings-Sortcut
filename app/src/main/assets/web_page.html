Enable Developer Mode

  1. Open Settings: Start by opening the Settings app on your Android device.
  2. Navigate to About Phone: Scroll down and select "About phone" or "About device."
  3. Find Build Number: Look for "Build number," which may be located under "Software information" or similar.
  4. Enable Developer Mode: Tap "Build number" multiple times (usually seven) until you see a message saying "You are now a developer!" You might be prompted to enter your device's PIN or password.

Accessing Developer Options

  1. Go Back to Settings: Return to the main Settings menu.
  2. Open Developer Options: You should now see a new menu item called "Developer options" or "System" -> "Developer options."

Using Developer Options

  • USB Debugging: Enable this to allow your device to communicate with a computer for development purposes.
  • OEM Unlocking: This allows you to unlock the bootloader, which is necessary for certain types of device modifications.
  • Stay Awake: Keeps the screen on while charging, useful for testing apps.
  • Show Taps: Displays visual feedback for touch interactions.
  • Limit Background Processes: Controls the number of processes that can run in the background.
Note: Changing settings in Developer Options can affect the performance and stability of your device. Only adjust settings if you understand their impact.

  • Force Stop: Stops the app from running. Use this if an app is unresponsive or behaving abnormally.
  • Notifications: Customize how the app sends notifications, including turning them off entirely.

  • Screen Time: View how much time you have spent using the app.
  • Battery & Performance: Check the app's battery usage and manage its background activity to save battery life.
  • Open by Default: Set or clear default actions for the app.

Additional Management Options

  1. Special App Access: In the "Apps & notifications" menu, select "Advanced" and then "Special app access" to manage specific permissions like battery optimization, device admin apps, and more.
  2. Default Apps: Set default apps for actions like browsing, messaging, and calling by selecting "Default apps" in the "Apps & notifications" menu.
  3. App Permissions: In the "Apps & notifications" menu, select "Permissions manager" to manage permissions for all apps from one place.
Note: Changing app settings can affect how apps behave on your device. Make sure to understand the impact of each setting before making changes.

Accessibility Settings

  1. Open Settings: Start by opening the Settings app on your Android device.
  2. Navigate to Accessibility: Scroll down and select "Accessibility."
  • Screen Reader: Enables TalkBack or a similar feature to provide spoken feedback for users with visual impairments.

Device Information

  1. Open Settings: Start by opening the Settings app on your Android device.
  2. Navigate to About Phone: Scroll down and select "About phone" or "About device" depending on your device.

Understanding Device Information

  • Device Name: The name of your device, which can usually be customized.
  • Model Number: The specific model number of your device.
  • Android Version: The version of the Android operating system your device is running.
  • Build Number: The build number of the software currently installed on your device. This can be useful for troubleshooting and technical support.
  • Kernel Version: The version of the Linux kernel used by Android on your device.
  • Baseband Version: Information about the baseband version, which handles communication functions like cellular, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth.
  • Security Patch Level: Indicates the date of the latest security patch applied to your device. Keeping this updated helps protect your device from vulnerabilities.
  • IP Address: The IP address assigned to your device when connected to a network.
  • MAC Address: The unique hardware address of your device’s network interface (usually for Wi-Fi and Bluetooth).
  • IMEI Number: A unique identifier for your device used by mobile networks to identify valid devices. Often needed for customer support and warranty claims.

Additional Device Information

  1. Battery Information: View details about your battery, such as its health, status, and level.
  2. Storage Information: Check the available and used storage on your device.
  3. Memory Information: See the amount of RAM available and used by the device.
  4. Legal Information: Review legal information related to your device, including open-source licenses and regulatory information.
Note: Knowing your device information can be helpful for troubleshooting issues, understanding your device's capabilities, and providing information for technical support.

User Dictionary Settings

  1. Open Settings: Start by opening the Settings app on your Android device.
  2. Navigate to System: Scroll down and select "System."
  3. Language & Input: Tap on "Language & input."
  4. Personal Dictionary: Select "Personal dictionary" or "User dictionary" to access the user dictionary settings.

Adding a New Word

  1. Add Word: Tap on the "+" or "Add" button to add a new word to the dictionary.
  2. Enter Word: Type the word you want to add to the dictionary.
  3. Shortcut (Optional): You can also add a shortcut for the word, which will allow you to type the shortcut and have it automatically replaced by the full word.
  4. Save: Tap "Save" or "Done" to add the word to your personal dictionary.

Editing or Deleting a Word

  1. Select Word: In the personal dictionary, tap on the word you want to edit or delete.
  2. Edit Word: To edit, modify the word or shortcut and save the changes.
  3. Delete Word: To delete, tap on the delete icon (usually a trash can) to remove the word from your dictionary.

Using the User Dictionary

  1. Typing: When you type a word that is in your personal dictionary, your keyboard will suggest it as you type.
  2. Shortcuts: If you added shortcuts, typing the shortcut will automatically replace it with the full word, making typing quicker and easier.
Note: The user dictionary helps improve typing efficiency by allowing you to add frequently used words and shortcuts. Customize it to suit your typing habits.
